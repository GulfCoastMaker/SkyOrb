✈ SkyOrb
Live flight radar for ESP32 · Round 1.28" GC9A01 display

What You Get

📡Live ADS-B flight tracking via airplanes.live (free, no API key)
🟢Animated radar sweep with glowing aircraft blips
🕐Second display: digital clock with auto timezone
📱Configure everything from your phone — set location, radius, and airports
🌍Airport markers auto-found via OpenStreetMap
📶Zero-config WiFi setup — no code editing required

What You Need

• 30-pin ESP32 dev board (ESP-WROOM-32) (like this one)
• Two GC9A01 round 1.28" displays (240×240 SPI, 7-pin) (like these) — one for radar, one for the clock
• 30-pin ESP32 GPIO breakout board with screw terminals (like this one) — lets you connect everything with screw terminals, no soldering required

Wiring (radar display):
SCL→D18   SDA→D23   RES→D4   DC→D2   CS→D5   VCC→3.3V   GND→GND

Wiring (clock display):
SCL→D18   SDA→D23 (shared)   RES→D21   DC→TX2   CS→RX2   VCC→3.3V   GND→GND

Install Firmware

1
Use Google Chrome or Microsoft Edge on a desktop or laptop — this won't work on mobile or Firefox.
2
Plug your ESP32 into a USB port and wait a few seconds for it to be detected.
3
Click Install SkyOrb below, select your ESP32's COM port, and follow the prompts. Flashing takes a couple of minutes.
4
After flashing, your ESP32 opens a hotspot called SKYORB-Setup (password: radar1234). On your phone, connect to that hotspot — a setup page pops up. Choose your home WiFi from the list and enter its password, then tap Save.
5
Important — switch your phone back to your home WiFi. After saving, your phone usually stays connected to the SKYORB-Setup hotspot. Go into your phone's WiFi settings and reconnect to your normal home network. (Your phone may do this automatically after a minute, but it's faster to switch it yourself.) If you skip this, the next step won't load.
6
Now — with your phone on your home WiFi — open http://skyorb.local in a browser to set your location, radius, and airports. The radar display also shows this address on screen at startup, with a backup IP address if skyorb.local doesn't load.
Requires Chrome or Edge on Windows / Mac / Linux.
Not supported on iOS, Android, or Firefox.
